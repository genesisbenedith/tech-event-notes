## Official Session Description

Artificial Intelligence has immense potential to increase fairness, mitigate bias, expand opportunity, and enhance well-being. It also carries significant risks and tradeoffs. This session explored AI policies, practices, and use-cases that organizations, institutions, and individuals can use to make sure AI is a force for maximum good. AI is already reshaping decisions across workplaces, markets, and institutions — from talent systems and performance evaluations to lending, risk assessment, and everyday digital interactions.

**You'll leave with:**
- Best practices for ensuring AI mitigates, rather than reinforces, human biases in common use cases
- Insight into where risk enters automated decision-making systems
- Practical ways leaders can shape responsible AI adoption inside organizations
- A framework for thinking about AI's role in expanding long-term economic opportunity

**About this session:** Impact, Legacy, & Expanding Opportunity

## Overview

This panel explored how AI can be harnessed for good while actively avoiding the encoding of bias and inequity into systems that impact real people — particularly underrepresented and marginalized communities.

## Hiring, ATS & Systemic Exclusion

- **Agency, dignity, and participation** must be centered

- Recruiters at companies are using AI in **ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)** to **exclude zip codes** in racially segregated community areas *(marginalization)*
  - Hiring processes **should not have AI automation** that affects decisions based on race, disability, and more
    - ❓ *What happened to HUMAN resources?* 😂

- **Everything with AI needs to be designed AND redesigned WITH** [people, communities, the excluded]
